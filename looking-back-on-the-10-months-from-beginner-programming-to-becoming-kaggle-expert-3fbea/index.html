##4.2 Kaggle debut (1-2 months) When I learned that there was a data science competition called Kaggle, I thought, “I think it’s the best to be able to combine study and profit (prize)!”, and immediately registered.

I was shocked by the very grateful fact that the solution was shared. But when machine learning & programs that aren’t even understandable are written in English, laymen can be a bit teary: fearful:

At that time, there was a competition in which various Japanese people helped, including Qiita. Yes, it’s Titanic

Titanic competition

It is a competition that is always held in Kaggle. It is a competition that predicts who will survive among the people who board the Titanic (various people with different age, gender, status).

Since it was my first time doing everything, it was a pain just to manipulate the table data. Of course it’s my first time with pandas. However, **You can have a complete experience of data cleansing, feature analysis, model building, validation, and inference. ** There is a wealth of information online, so I learned a lot. We also implemented decision trees and random forests for the first time. I think there was no loss in doing it.

“I see machine learning, I see,” I was in good shape and immediately jumped into a prize-winning competition.

Kaggle Wire Competition

For me as a mechanic, the failure analysis system was an easy subject to tackle. It was also an interesting topic in terms of time series data that I often see.

For the first time I saw RNN, GBDT, etc., it was a battle of the gods. I am in a mood.

I did not hesitate to copy and paste the public solution (kernel). While submitting it while playing around with it little by little, the code that belonged to another person becomes more and more familiar. (However, as you add it, it becomes dirty code and the score does not change much) You just have to copy and paste and defeat it at first, surely.

I knew the result, but it was super bad. However, thanks to the kernel, I learned a lot from python coding to time series data analysis. Thank you to everyone who gave me the kernel.

Kaggle Earthquake Competition

I was in a good condition to have mastered the time series, and I will take part in a competition of the same series that happened immediately after. **This time I tried to reach the submission without looking at the open solution. **

##4.4 Intro to Table Competition (4th-5th month) When I noticed the importance of machine specs in an image competition and got tired of bad learning iterations, I challenged myself to a table competition. I had a bit of a bit of a time series competition, but I had no experience with ordinary table competitions.

Around that time, an interesting competition will be held at Signate.

Iida Sangyo Land Competition

It is a very basic competition that predicts the price of land properties in Saitama.

I was able to learn the joy of creating various feature quantities, such as calculating the time distance from longitude and latitude, and creating the area per room from the number of rooms. It becomes quite a habit if the features that flashed suddenly affect the accuracy**. I think this kind of place is very suitable for an idea man who is good at various thoughts and delusions. The substance in the brain comes out.

I was able to learn more about the importance of validation. I didn’t know much when I was told by Professor Andrew at Coursera, but poor validation can lead to contradictions when assessed in terms of accuracy. I think this kind of place is also a great knowledge that can be gained by the competition.By the way, it’s a bonus that you can (uselessly) learn more about Saitama.

I also took a sneak peek at the molecular competition, but I didn’t have much time after all. GNN looks fun and learning is a topic for the future.

##4.5 Image competition relapse (6-9 months) I’ve been studying detection tasks since the Tobacco Competition, just as Japan hosted a competition at Kaggle.

Kaggle Breakdown Competition

It was a task to detect and classify the broken letters. Detection itself was not such a hard task, so I tried to implement it myself. By this time, I was able to implement the paper a little, so I actively tried new things.

Thanks to kaggle for taking care of me, I also shared the solution with the kernel.

I faced the most convoluted neural net so far. If you are aware of the points you want to show, it seems that you can see the neural network properly. For the first time, I was also made to think seriously about the influence of context. Looking at The elephant in the room, I found CNN interesting. Interestingness was a big driving force, and we made various trials and errors.

The importance of validation, which I learned in the land competition, was also put to good use. If the same author’s documents were mixed in training and validation, the accuracy would be overestimated. Group Split was the correct answer.

It was a Kaggle competition, but it was a miraculous competition with no medal, so it was miraclely ranked 7th. Unfortunately, it’s out of the bounty zone, but I was given awards, which helped me to improve my motivation. https://www.kaggle.com/c/kuzushiji-recognition
