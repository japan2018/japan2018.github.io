Axiom of friendship

In general, when a political party [x, y] exists, it is expected that

  1. The political party [x, y] is friendly with y (because the former defends the latter).
  • Political party [x, y] is not friendly with x (because the former is trying to protect something from the latter).

The original article also assumes the following axioms:

  • If x and y are friendly and y and z are friendly, then x and z are also friendly (allies are allies)

Now, when we say that “x and y are friendly” as “x ~ y”, The above axiom can be paraphrased as “relationship ~ is transitive”.

In addition, the reflection temperament (I am friendly with myself) and the symmetry temperament (y and x are also friendly if x and y are friendly) are also considered to be true, so relationship ~ has an equivalence relationship.

Therefore, it can be seen that all political parties are divided into equal classes without duplication.

Is there a third power?

Now, it turns out that all political parties are nicely divided into some camp, How many camps can exist? Is there a “third power” that does not give to NHK or the people?

Now, from Axiom 1, [x,y] ~ y holds for any political party [x,y]. It is clear that the latter has less “number of brackets used for notation” than the former.

So you can find a friendly party (or NHK or national) with less brackets for any party.

And as we continue to find friendly parties that are less brazen than ourselves, You can see that eventually the parentheses are 0, that is, you will reach the NHK or the people.

Therefore, any political party [x, y] must be friendly with either the NHK or the people, You can see that there is no third power because it is included in either equivalence class.

Conflicting manifest

According to Axiom 2, [x, y] !~ x holds for any political party [x, y], This means that x belongs to the camp that [x, y] does not belong to, since all parties belong to the NHK or national camps according to the above discussion.

If [x, y] belongs to both camps at this time, it is inconsistent because x does not belong to either camp.

Therefore, there can be no political party on the side of both NHK and the people in a consistent world, It also follows that if there is any political party NHK and the people must be hostile.

To put it the other way around, it means that in a world where NHK and the people are friendly, no political parties are needed.

#Faction function

A function that assigns a faction to any political party in a friendly world

T(x): x \mapsto \{ \{0\}, \{1\}, \{0, 1\} \}

Can be defined.

At this time, for any political party [x, y],

T(x) \cap T(y) \neq \varnothing \Rightarrow T([x, y]) = \{0, 1\} \\
T(x) \cap T(y) = \varnothing \Rightarrow T([x, y]) = T(y)

You can see that

In other words, whether or not the political party [x, y] is inconsistent in the original world can be determined by recursively examining the factions in the easy world.

#Judge with Python

Keep the contents of the previous section as a program.

def T(party):
  if party in [0, 1]:
    return [party]
  else:
    p = T(party[0])
    q = T(party[1])
    if len(set(p) & set(q)) != 0:
      return [0, 1]
    else:
      return q

Addendum A: How Many Political Parties Are Consistent?

Now define the political party rank R(x) as follows.

R(0) = R(1) = 0, \\
R(x, y) = max(R(x), R(y)) + 1

For example, $$R([1, 1]) = max(0, 0) + 1 = 1$$.

Rank 0 is only two, NHK and the people, and both are not contradictory (aside from the actual situation).

There are four rank 1 political parties, [0, 0], [0, 1], [1, 0], [1, 1], of which two are not inconsistent.

It can be seen that there are 6 * 6 = 36 pieces because the rank 2 political party is obtained as a combination of 6 groups with rank 1 or lower. In addition, there are two NHK camps and two national camps in a consistent party of rank 1 or lower, and a political party that is not inconsistent can be created only in combination with the enemy camp. It can be seen that there are 4 * 2 = 8 consistent party of rank 2.

Similarly, it is clear that there are (2 + 4 + 36)^2 = 1764 parties of rank 3 and only 12 * 6 = 72 of them are consistent.

This difference opens up more and more as the rank increases, so it can be said that ** almost all political parties in the world are inconsistent**.
