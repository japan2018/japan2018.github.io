Report from the site. What I think is necessary to utilize AI/IoT in the manufacturing industry

Introduction

As is said to be the “cliff of 2025,” digital transformation is an urgent issue for the manufacturing industry. While emerging countries are chasing after quality and cost, I feel the harshness of Japan at the factory. I wonder, “Is Japanese quality really good?” (Maybe it’s just my factory!). However, for Japan, whose population is declining, it is clear that it is necessary to manufacture high value-added products, and AI/IoT in particular is a major touchstone for that. I would like to convey the feeling of the site from the person who is in charge of AI/IoT at the manufacturer (smiley w).

I would like to say the following three points. ・It is useless to create an AI/IoT promotion section only for the headquarters mechanism ・Shopping alone is not enough, it is necessary to raise the overall programming level ・Existing IT personnel may become an obstacle

It is useless to create an AI/IoT promotion section only for the headquarters mechanism

What many large companies do is to set up an AI/IoT specialized department in a headquarters structure that is separate from the factory. I think this is a pattern that will not work if it is made only for the headquarters. I think that what is important for utilizing AI is high quality data and software customized on site. In that respect, I think it is extremely difficult for a person at the headquarters to obtain new data without knowing the existing data of the factory. In addition, even if they start collecting new data, they can’t deal with the troubles that occur every day and leave it to the factory. Headquarters “Can’t analyze because good data doesn’t come up” Factory “I don’t think how difficult it is to get data!” Is likely to be. After all, it’s not enough to set up a promotion group only at the headquarters, so it’s no use unless there is a main team in charge at the factory. I think setting up a group at the headquarters is good for itself, but I think it would be impossible for the headquarters alone to succeed.

Shopping alone is not enough, needs to raise overall programming level

I think there are many things that tend to be done after shopping. Hey Tableau, Hey DataRobot, and other high-priced products such as DataSpider and SensorCorpus that make us happy with AI/IoT. I think this is really wasteful in the sense that I cannot master it. Customization is required to utilize it in daily manufacturing, and you must write the code yourself somewhere. I think it is possible to buy a tool or drop one hand without understanding this necessity.

Existing IT personnel may be a barrier

I want this to be the best of the three. It’s been nearly 30 years since a factory built in the bubble era. At our factories, that’s about it, but what’s common in these factories is the legacyization of systems. For example, my factory uses Oracle, but I get angry when I connect to the database with SQL. Existing IT staff “What should I do if the connection becomes unstable?” “…” I want to get angry by connecting to Oracle with a command and issuing a select statement with SQL (click and click on GUI software from 30 years ago to get the data). .. AI development would be difficult in this situation. Even if only I issue a select statement after the end of a frustration, if there is anything, all new code will be scapegoat, so it is a day-to-day affair. I am sorry to write a bat file that connects to Oracle, but it is not true. .. You’re doing bad things! ? In addition, the bad point is that the knowledge of the existing personnel is one generation ago. Even if you have a problem and explain “I don’t know Python!”

Countermeasure

Based on the above, the measures I need to take in order to utilize AI/IoT in the manufacturing industry are described below.

It is useless to create an AI/IoT promotion section only at the headquarters

→ I am very helpful to the headquarters. But I think that the factory side also needs a team.

  1. In charge of infrastructure that ensures data quality,
  2. In charge of the backend that manages the database and API,
  3. And in charge of the front-end that customizes according to the work of production technology
    Is required. It may be okay to let the headquarters develop common algorithm development, but I think that a main team is needed in the factory.
Existing IT personnel may get in the way

→ It’s tough. It’s easy to say clearly. To avoid trouble, it would be ideal if the data line, database, and server were completely separated for AI/IoT. The database can be MySQL, the server can be GCP or AWS, and a PC can manage it. The data line can be divided by Second Ether. Still, when I was told that other software stopped due to heavy data traffic, I laugh and cry in my heart.

The bottom of the whole needs to be raised

Everyone needs to understand programming. It’s hard to get rooted among people who think they are irrelevant, but I think the staff needs some understanding. I think it’s good to have students study under the name of “tools that automate fixed procedures”.

###Finally It would be wonderful if I could master AI/IoT, but I think there are many manufacturing issues in Japan. It’s old with something attached, and it’s stuck. .. It would be easy to recreate from the factory! What? Ah, the emerging countries have already won. …No! !
