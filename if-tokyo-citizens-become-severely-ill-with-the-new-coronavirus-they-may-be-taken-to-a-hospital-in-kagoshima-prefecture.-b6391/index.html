#Introduction

  • Background Infectious disease designated medical institutions are tight, and it is extremely difficult to secure sufficient beds to accommodate severely ill persons. In various places, measures have been taken to treat patients with mild illness at hotels, homes, etc. in order to secure beds for those with severe illness.
  • Task
    With the spread of new coronavirus infections, it is important to how quickly the severely ill persons who occur at the same time can be transported to designated medical institutions for infectious diseases. In addition, it is desirable to keep the distance as short as possible in order to avoid the risk of infection caused by transportation and further aggravation.
  • Purpose
    -To understand the current risk of medical collapse by visualizing the tightness of infectious disease designated medical institutions due to the spread of new coronavirus infection -Judging whether or not a bed can be secured to accept severely ill persons when all mildly ill persons are treated at a hotel or home -To construct a mathematical model that minimizes the total transport distance of the critically ill and consider its behavior -Based on the above analysis results, increase or decrease one’s sense of crisis for the new coronavirus (** In conclusion, Tokyo residents were transported to Kagoshima prefecture, and the sense of crisis exploded.)

#Severelyilltransportmodel From the simulation results, it turned out that the atmosphere was awful. Next, I’m worried about what to do for seriously ill persons who could not be admitted to a designated infectious disease medical institution in their area. This time, it is a bit of a bit of a bad idea, but we will consider transporting it to another prefecture. There may be a lot of pros and cons, but there is a lot of pros and cons, Trolley problem I had no choice but to do this.

In order to avoid infection risk and further aggravation due to transportation, it is considered that the distance should be as short as possible, so we will consider a mathematical model that minimizes the total transportation distance of severely ill persons.

If you are interested in the details of the mathematical model, click here
  • The critically ill person set is $I$ and the infectious disease designated medical institution set is $J$.
  • Carry infectious disease designated medical institution $ j(\in J)$ to the critically ill person $ i(\in I)$.
  • The distance when the critically ill $i$ moves to the designated infectious disease medical institution $j$ is $d_{ij}$.
  • If severely ill $i$ is assigned to infectious disease designated medical institution $j$, $x_{ij}=1$, otherwise $x_{ij}=0$.
  • Find $x_{ij}$ that minimizes the total transport distance.
  • The maximum number of beds $C_j$ at the designated infectious disease medical institution $j$ shall not be exceeded.
  • All critically ill persons will be sent to $j$, a designated medical institution for infectious diseases.

As a numerical experiment, we optimize the delivery destination of 19% of severely ill patients randomly selected from all infected people. However, because there is insufficient data on location information, there is only one location information for severely ill persons in the prefectural office of the place of residence, one infectious disease designated medical institution in each prefecture, and the distance is Vincenty Law
