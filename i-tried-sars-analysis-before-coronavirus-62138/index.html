import pandas as pd
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

###2.CSV output

df1 = pd.read_csv('analytics_SARS].csv')
df1.head()

###3. Look at the increase in the number of infected people

x_num = df1['day']
y_num = df1['total']
plt.plot(x_num,y_num)#show graph
Up to about 60 days, there was an upward trend, but from there it can be seen that the number of infected persons has decreased significantly. ###4.1 Look at the change in the number of infected people per day 
x_num = df1['day']
y_num = df1['total-change']
plt.plot(x_num,y_num)#show graph
It can be predicted that the initial fluctuations are due to gradual new reports of infection from new countries. ###5. Look at the increase in deaths 
x_num = df1['day']
y_num = df1['death']
plt.plot(x_num,y_num)#show graph
800 people have died in about 100 days. In other words, as many as eight people are dead in one simple calculation day.

###6 Look at the number of deaths per day

x_num = df1['day']
y_num = df1['death-change']
plt.plot(x_num,y_num)#show graph
The number of deaths per day is increasing and decreasing. This seems to be due to the cycle of arrival of death news. ###### Mortality 
 df1['death'].sum() / df1['total'].sum()

You can see that the mortality rate is 0.08121591227553301, which is about 8%.

7. Look at the number of recovered people

x_num = df1['day']
y_num = df1['recovery']
plt.plot(x_num,y_num)#show graph
It can be seen that the recoverers gradually appeared from around 20 days. The increase in the number of people who recovered from that was remarkable.

###8.1 Look at the change in the number of infected people per day

x_num = df1['day']
y_num = df1['recovery_change']
plt.plot(x_num,y_num)#show graph
A sharp rise is seen around 20 days. This seems to be related to the number of recovered persons in each country. However, the number of recovered persons seems to be stable on average. #Summary This time, I focused on simple and easy analysis.
