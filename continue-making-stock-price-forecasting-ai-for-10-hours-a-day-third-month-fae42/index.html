Third month

By the second month I’ve faced quite a few problems. There seems to be a limit to maximizing the prediction accuracy of one AI. Therefore, I came up with a method that may improve the prediction accuracy, so I will write it here.

##AI majority theory The method is simple, first create some good AI, preferably an odd number of AIs. After that, AI will be used to make a majority decision and make a final decision. In the first place, there is an aspect that most of the AIs make predictions by majority voting, so it is doubtful whether actually gathering multiple AIs and making judgment will be effective, but let’s do it anyway. The problem is that the AIs collected should probably be diverse, plus some number. This month’s goal was to create some high-performance predictive AI using various data.

Problems in learning

There are two problems in learning the data. One is over-learning, and the other problem is bias in prediction. For example, if you define 0 to go up and 1 to go down, the model so far frequently outputs 0 (0 to 70% to 100% of all predictions). The tricky part of these problems is that trying to solve the problem of overfitting leads to biased predictions and vice versa. The following three variables were mainly involved in this issue: 1. Normalization Layers setting strength and number of layers. 2. Number of epochs. 3. Select Optimizer In addition, other than the learning process, the factors that influence the results are 4. Data structure and quality. And 5. The structure of the model.

About AI research

On rare occasions, you can accidentally discover a good combination of the above variables, resulting in a good or very good AI. Deep learning is not just about learning the data, but also requires various adjustments and trial and error in the process, and I think that there is room for research here and improvement for the library side that makes AI. We have created 3 types of AI so far, but the performance of the 2nd and 3rd AI is better than the 1st AI, and we are considering removing the 1st AI from Congress. #AI_No.2

This is the performance of the second AI. There is a 65.6% prediction accuracy for all stock price data (43707) from 500 major US companies in the year 2000, and the output ratio of 0 (prediction that the stock price will rise) is 57%, which is a particular problem. There is none. And the expected accuracy of 51.56% when the prediction is completely random suggests that the prediction is quite powerful.
