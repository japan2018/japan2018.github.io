I tried fitting exponential function and logistics function to the number of COVID-19 positive patients in Tokyo

Apr 3, 2020 Python DataScience Corona Coronavirus COVID-19 

#Background

There was a person who fitted the trend of the number of COVID-19 infected people in the world to an exponential function and a logistic function and calculated the doubling period (https://github.com/aatishb/covid), so I cloned it. I applied it to the data on the number of patients published by Tokyo.

When it comes to Tokyo, the difference in the situation depending on the region is mixed into one distribution if it is national data, so it is easier to fit it to an ideal infection explosion model in a limited area. .. It is also the most risky area in Japan.

Analysis method and notes are written in Notebook.

#Result

The Notebook is here: https://github.com/msakuta/covid/blob/master/curvefit-tokyo.ipynb

** Based on Most Recent Week of Data **

Confirmed cases on 2020-04-02 00:00:00 587.0
Confirmed cases on 2020-03-26 00:00:00 212.0
Ratio: 2.77
Weekly increase: 176.9%
Daily increase: 15.7 %per day
Doubling Time (represents recent growth): 4.8 days

** Based on Logistic Fit**

R^2: 0.9753797360404987
Doubling Time (during middle of growth): 12.02 (± nan )days

** Based on Exponential Fit **

R^2: 0.975379736914424
Doubling Time (represents overall growth): 6.01 (± 0.38) days

Of note is the value of R^2, which is above 0.97 for both exponential and logistic functions, and can be seen to follow a typical explosive growth trajectory.

The doubling period is 4.8 days, the logistic function is 12.02 days (at the maximum slope point), and the exponential function is 6.01 based on the number of cases in the last week.

When extrapolated

Extrapolating the exponential function is generally not a good idea, but if exponential growth continues, it will be around 20,000 after 30 days.

Of course, it is not easy to conclude that such a transition occurs. The city is also requesting to refrain from going out, and people’s perceptions have changed significantly over the past few weeks. I think there is a good chance that it will converge soon. However, looking at this graph, I can’t help thinking that I’m just stepping on because it’s quite noisy.

The scary thing about COVID-19 is that it has an involuntary incubation period during which it may spread the virus. According to the Japanese Society of Infectious Diseases (https://www.kansensho.or.jp/ref/d77.html), this incubation period is 1 to 14 days, but it is the longest period after taking countermeasures until it becomes effective. It means that there is a delay of about two weeks. Personally, I think it’s too late to issue an emergency declaration.

Also, the number of tests in Japan is considerably smaller than in other countries, so it is unknown how many undetected infected people are lurking.

The more difficult part is that the situation changes drastically every week, so I think there are some areas where ordinary people’s senses are not catching up. However, even in Tokyo, one month later, like in New York, the bodies overflowing from the hospital may be stored in the refrigerator.

Fortunately, the number of infected people is updated daily, so I would like to watch trends as much as possible.
