Perhaps there is a problem with the verification data itself.
Let’s find out where preprocessing and data processing are performed.

Discard horses that do not have the same results in the past five runs

Here is where I got caught

# Delete missing information line
df = df.dropna(subset=[
    'past_time_sec1','past_time_sec2','past_time_sec3',
    'past_time_sec4','past_time_sec5'
]).reset_index(drop=True)

past_time_sec1 ~ past_time_sec5 represents the time of the past 5 runs of the horse. This means that horses that don’t have all the time for the past five runs are abandoned here. Especially in the ages of 2-3, there are variations in the number of runs for each horse. For example, if you were Fukushima 10R Fukushima 2-year-old S on November 10, 2019 last week, there are 14 runners (https://race.netkeiba.com/?pid=race_old&id=c201903030410), but the time for the past 5 runs has been gathered. There are 3 of them, and actually there were only 3 in the dataframe after deletion. With this dropna, the number of records is 471500 -> 252885 **. **About half of the data is discarded. ** The data discarded here are the horses that are 2-3 years old, the local horse (because the local horse race has not acquired the data), and the 2010 data (there is no data for 2009, so the previous race information cannot be acquired). It was the center. It doesn’t seem right, but it’s not a fatal mistake because it can be excluded by the same rule during inference.

How many wins will be refunded for a win?

If the number of races is 8 or more, up to 3 will be won, if it is 5 to 7, 2 will be won, and if it is 4 or less, it will not be sold. The following processing was done **for verification data.

# Focus on races with 2 or more double wins and 5 or more all

By this process, the number of records becomes 48555 -> 42999, and 11.4% of the data is discarded. Originally I would like to throw away the race that did not have a multiple win refund, but it is too many to think about. In fact, no more than 4 races in JRA in 2018-2019 (at least in my keibadb) This process is a problem.

#What’s going on What’s wrong? Since win3 is the objective variable that indicates whether or not the player has entered the finish order within the multi-win zone, the above process counts two or more horses in the multi-win zone and five or more horses in the race. But please remember. Horses that don’t have consistent results over the past five runs have already been discarded, so horses that shouldn’t be erased have disappeared**. It’s a bit confusing, but let’s look at it in detail. For example, Kyoto 12R on November 2, 2019. https://race.netkeiba.com/?pid=race&id=p201908050112&mode=shutuba Records of the 4th Boccherini, 7th Sunray Pocket, 9th Narita Blue, 10th Theo Amazon, and 12th Metropole have been excluded in advance because the race times of the last 5 races are not complete, and 8 of them stand up. It will be regarded as a race. The finish order for this race was 4-3-7. Since it is a race with 13 heads, the winning combination is 4, 3 or 7. However, since *4 and 7 horses have already been erased from the DataFrame, it means that only one horse has won the race in this race**. It will be one and will be excluded from the verification data. **This operation cannot be done for future races as we obviously don’t know which horse will be a winning ticket before the race** By the way, the 5th Nihon Piro Halo of this race lost the win odds of 60 times, but I lost it (in terms of verification data, I did not have to buy one horse to lose at 55 to 60 times). I can see it little by little. It seems that some horses are disappearing from the verification data regardless of the learning model, and they seem to be biased.
