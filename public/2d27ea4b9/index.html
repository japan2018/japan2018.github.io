#What is “Kaggle”?

What is Kaggle

In a word, it is the Tenkaichi Budokai of data analysis using “machine learning”.

Data to analyze

  • Company provides data and theme, holds competition
  • There are various themes such as medical care, marketing, finance

Competition period

  • About 1 to 3 months
  • Relatively long

Rank

  • Create a machine learning model and rank it according to the prediction results of that model

Prize money

  • There are also competitions where prize money is given to the top players
  • As of 2019/12, the 1st place in the DSB competition being held is $100,000 (about 10 million yen!)

Entry fee

  • free!

Registered user

  • Approximately 120,000 (as of December 2019) *Reference source

About rank

There are five ranks in Kaggle, depending on their strength. (Strictly speaking, in addition to the strength in the competition, you can also get the rank by contributing to the Kaggle community such as Kernels, Discussion, Datasets)

  • Grandmaster * The strongest…!
  • Master
  • Expert
  • Contributor
  • Novice *First from here

It is no exaggeration to say that the people who work on Kaggle (aka Kaggler) spend a great deal of time every day trying to improve their ranks in their competition.

  • Titanic: Machine Learning from Disaster
  • “Hello World” competition at Kaggle
  • There is no end to the period because it is a practice competition

Benefits of doing # Kaggle

I have listed three advantages of doing Kaggle.

① Know your data science skills (also proof)

Data science skills are difficult to measure unless practice is applied. However, if you have won medals and ranks at Kaggle, you can prove your approximate data science skills. Recently, the number of companies that have adopted Kaggle is gradually increasing, and it is also evidence when stepping up.

② Learn the know-how of world data scientists (for free!)

I myself learned that I participated in the competition, but Kaggle has a wonderful culture of sharing know-how and information as well as competing with others. Especially, in “Notebooks”, know-how and trends of data science are explained in an easy-to-understand manner along with the code, which is recommended for beginners.

③ You can understand what machine learning is doing at the level of skin feeling

Nowadays, machine learning is more and more embedded in everyday applications and marketing tools. If you are doing Kaggle, you will be able to master the modeling process of machine learning, so you can understand what you can and cannot do with machine learning.

Kaggle should start here

There may be disagreements, but there are three personal points below.

① Machine learning/Python If you have zero knowledge, select “Matsuo Lab” textbook!

Matsuo Lab of the University of Tokyo, which is famous for machine learning/AI related.

You can download the text of “Data Scientist Training Course” conducted by Matsuo Laboratory for free. This is amazing, the case! The text and Python code carefully explain the graduation level of beginners in machine learning, so if you have difficulty understanding the contents of Kaggle, I think you should complete this.

▼"Matsuo Lab” text Download here https://weblab.t.u-tokyo.ac.jp/gci_contents/

② Copy the ones with the highest number of votes in “Notebooks” and get the knack!

As I mentioned earlier, the “Notebooks” in each competition are a treasure trove of treasure.

If you choose a competition that seems easy to work on, it is recommended that you copy a notebook with a high number of “Notebooks” Votes. Notebooks with a high number of votes are carefully selected with easy-to-understand explanations, so you can relive the flow from data acquisition to model result submission by copying, and you can get the knack of machine learning. #### ③ It is recommended that you connect with someone, because if you are alone, there will be no information.

Kaggle is a basic individual game. However, the competition period is as long as 1 to 3 months, and the motivation may be lowered or it may be bogged down.

At that time, if you have a friend who is doing Kaggle, you can share useful information (private sharing is NG) and encourage each other, and you can enjoy Kaggle more.

The following three are personally recommended.

  • Go to Kaggle related seminars (connpass etc.)
  • Follow Kaggle on Twitter
  • Loosely join the Kaggle community
