What is machine learning?

I suppose everyone has such an experience.

** “I don’t know what I learned from machine learning” ** ** “Logistics regression, decision tree, support vector machine, too many methods” **

One of the pain points when learning machine learning is the number of techniques to remember. It is necessary to understand 5 to 10 methods just by reading one reference book.

What’s more, “how” and “how” to use these methods ** I have never met a reference book that has been carefully explained.

On the contrary, why not understand why there are various methods ** and their common points **? That person can understand the whole picture of machine learning with speed.

A purpose common to all machine learning. It means making a good model.

As you can see in the diagram, this is all about machine learning. “** Give a large amount of data to a computer and find out the rules for making predictions **”

However, there are various kinds of data in the world.

  • CSV (Excel): Sales data, customer data
  • Image: Photos posted on Instagram, video from surveillance cameras
  • Voice: Conference voice memo, call center call recording

Whatever the data, machine learning engineers are required to create a good model (precise prediction). There are various methods available as tool boxes for that purpose.

  • Sales data ⇒ Regression (multiple regression, regression tree, etc.)
  • Instagram photo ⇒ CNN-based method (VGG16, LesNet, etc.)
  • Voice memo ⇒ Time series analysis (RNN, LSTM, etc.)

When compared to cooking, the common goal of all is to make delicious food**. For that purpose, it is the same as using the appropriate **recipe (=method)** according to the **material (=data)**.

Organizing terms related to machine learning

Now that you can see the purpose of machine learning, let’s broaden our horizons.

As the first struggle in machine learning, AI / Machine Learning / Deep Learning It is an impression that many people cannot understand these differences.

If you read the 2018 news and articles where AI (machine learning) became popular, regarding these three I remember that there were many sentences that I felt, “I wonder if the person who wrote it doesn’t understand much?”

If you do not understand the difference here, even if you study hard This is an important issue because it is not systematically organized as usable knowledge.

First, the three terms AI/machine learning/deep learning have a comprehensive relationship as shown in the figure.

(Source: https://www.shikaku-square.com/media/ai-license/001-how-to-study-deep-learning-for-general/)

As you can see, it is a comprehensive relationship of “AI> machine learning> deep learning”. The words are as follows.

  • AI (Artificial Intelligence ): -A concept born from the first AI boom in the 1950s -The purpose is to artificially reproduce human intelligence (Intelligence) on a computer.
  • Machine learning: -A concrete method considered for realizing AI -The purpose is to find the law from a large amount of data and predict the future
  • Deep learning -Among machine learning, it is a field that uses a technology called ** neural network ** -The purpose is to automate the most important (and time-consuming) AI development process called feature extraction

By valuing this kind of basic knowledge, you will deepen your understanding of articles and reference books. Also, when it comes to machine learning projects, it becomes a matter of course for non-programmers to know.
